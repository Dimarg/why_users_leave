Why you should consider responsive design for your website:

A single URL/HTML code;
Faster website loading time;
Responsive design is responsive to all devices. Websites with responsive design are designed to respond to various screen sizes, not device sizes, so that users will be able to easily view your site on both current and future devices—no matter what the size;
Ease of use. No pinching and zooming. No side scrolling;
Boost conversions and reduce bounce rate;
Google loves responsive design.

Create great content

In order to appeal to the audience, the content displayed on the website needs to be original, trustworthy, engaging, short and pointed
Make a list of every feature of your product or service and ask yourself why each feature is included in the first place. Take the “why” and ask “how” does this connect with the prospect’s desires. Get to the absolute root of what’s in it for the prospect at an emotional level.
Focus on strong headlines: 80% of users read headlines and only 20% your content. Use the formula of the 4 Us:useful, ultra-specific, unique and urgent
Interactive content works the best: spend time developing videos, quizzes and interactive infographics
Update your website daily or at least 3-4 times a week and try user generated content
Use psychological pricing strategies: ​prices ending in 9 connote “value” price, prices ending in 0 now connote a “prestigious” price
Simplify the numbers when possible since longer prices appear to be more expensive for consumers than shorter prices, even if they represent the same number
